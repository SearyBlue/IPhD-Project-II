Define \il{x \equiv \ex{\fr{T^*}{T}} = \ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*}}}. 
The given expression for susceptibility in Haldane's paper is
\beq
\chi = \fr{\beta}{2(x + 2)} + \chi_0
\eeq
This is a partial attempt to derive this expression. The impurity level is fixed at \il{k_B T^*}. So, under a magnetic field,
\beq
\epsilon_\ua &= k_B T^* - h\\
\epsilon_\da &= k_B T^* + h
\eeq
The magnetization is
\beq
m = n_\ua - n_\da &= n(\epsilon_\ua) - n(\epsilon_\da)\\
		  &=\fr{1}{\ex{\beta \epsilon_\ua}+1} - \fr{1}{\ex{\beta \epsilon_\da}+1}\\
		  &=\fr{1}{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} - \beta h}+1} - \fr{1}{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} + \beta h}+1}
\eeq
Then,
\beq
\pd{m}{h} = -\fr{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} - \beta h}\rr{-\beta}}{\qq{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} - \beta h}+1}^2} + \fr{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} + \beta h}\beta}{\qq{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*} + \beta h}+1}^2}
\eeq
Susceptibility is
\beq
\chi = \lim_{h \to 0} \pd{m}{h} &= -\fr{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*}}\rr{-\beta}}{\qq{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*}}+1}^2} + \fr{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*}}\beta}{\qq{\ex{\fr{\beta}{\beta^*}}+1}^2}\\
				&=\beta \fr{2x}{\rr{1+x}^2}\\
				&= \fr{2\beta}{x + 2 + x^{-1}}
\eeq
Its quite close. Any ideas on improving this?
